# BURNOUT (GAD221 Project)

## Summary
Burnout is a rapid-fire game in which you find yourself playing as a college
student trying to maintain a high GPA without going insane. You need to collect
study material, triggering mini games, and the level of stress increases. The stress
can be reduced by collecting leisure items, which reduce your stress levels.
However, if you collect too many leisure items, then you will reach a fatigue state,
which reduces the movement speed and the effect of leisure items.

## Gameplay Mechanics
- Movement: Player A and D keys to catch falling assignments and self care items and Mouse for Playing Minigames.
- Mini-Games: Each assignment triggers a short, time-limited task, finishing the mini game increases gpa, but increases stress as well.
- GPA System: Successful tasks increase GPA, once it reaches the maximum you win the game.
- Timer System: Minigames increase the timer after each play, if the timer reaches 0, you lose.
- Stress System: Once stress reaches maximum, the game is over and you lose.
- Burnout State: At 100% stress, player loses and fails.
- Self-Care Items: Reduce stress and help maintain performance.
- Fatigue: Over use of self care items may cause fatigue to increase resulting in decreased effectiveness of self care items.

## Design Intent
The mechanics are designed to directly reflect the experience of academic burnout:
- The mini-games represent the effort required to complete academic tasks.
- The GPA vs Stress system forces players to balance productivity and wellbeing.
- Visual and gameplay effects (darkening screen, slower movement) simulate mental fatigue and exhaustion.
- Self-care mechanics reinforce the message that rest and balance are necessary for success.
